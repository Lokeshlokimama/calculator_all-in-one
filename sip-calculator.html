What this SIP calculator does

A Systematic Investment Plan, or SIP, is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly, usually every month. This calculator estimates how a monthly investment may grow over a selected period if it earns a chosen annual return. It separates the amount you invest from the estimated returns so you can understand the role of compounding.

Enter your monthly investment, expected annual return, and the number of years you plan to invest. The calculator assumes monthly compounding and that each monthly contribution is made consistently. Longer time periods can have a large effect because earlier investments get more time to compound. Increasing the monthly amount can also significantly raise the final value.

Investment returns are not guaranteed. Mutual funds, equity funds, and market-linked investments can rise or fall depending on market conditions, fees, taxes, exit loads, and fund performance. Use this page for scenario planning, not as financial advice. To plan responsibly, compare conservative, moderate, and optimistic return assumptions and make sure your investment amount fits your cash flow and risk tolerance.

SIP formula

Future value = P x (((1 + r)^n - 1) / r) x (1 + r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly return rate, and n is total months.

Example calculation

If you invest Rs 5,000 per month for 10 years at an estimated 12% annual return, the invested amount is Rs 6,00,000 and the estimated maturity value is about Rs 11,61,695.

FAQs

Are SIP returns guaranteed?

No. Market-linked investments do not guarantee returns. The calculator uses an assumed annual return.

Can I use this for mutual funds?

Yes. It is commonly used for mutual fund SIP planning.

Does it include tax?

No. Taxes, exit loads, and fund expenses are not included in the result.

What return should I enter?

Use a reasonable assumption based on the investment type and your risk profile.
