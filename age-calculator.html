What this age calculator does

This age calculator converts a date of birth into an easy-to-read age result. Instead of showing only completed years, it breaks the difference into years, months, and days, which is useful for forms, school records, eligibility checks, birthday planning, documentation, and personal milestones.

The calculator compares your selected birth date with today. It subtracts years first, then adjusts months and days when the current day of the month is earlier than the birth day. This avoids the common mistake of simply dividing total days by 365, which can be inaccurate because months have different lengths and leap years add extra days.

You can also see the approximate total number of days lived. That number is useful for fun milestones, event planning, or comparing date differences. For official documents, always use the date format and rules required by the institution requesting the information. Some exams, government forms, or legal processes use a specific cut-off date instead of today's date, so verify the requirement before submitting.

Age formula

Age is calculated by subtracting the date of birth from today's date, then adjusting for unfinished months and days.

Example calculation

If someone was born on 10 June 2000 and today is 1 June 2026, they are 25 years, 11 months, and 22 days old because the 2026 birthday has not arrived yet.

FAQs

Does this include leap years?

Yes. JavaScript date handling accounts for leap years when calculating total days.

Can I calculate age for a future date?

This page calculates age as of today. For future eligibility dates, use the main date tools on the homepage.

Why are months and days adjusted?

They are adjusted because calendar months have different lengths and birthdays may not have occurred yet in the current month.

Is this valid for legal documents?

Use it as a helpful estimate, then confirm with the official date rules required by the document or authority.
