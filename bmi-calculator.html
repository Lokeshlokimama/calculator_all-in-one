What this BMI calculator does

Body Mass Index, commonly called BMI, is a quick screening number based on your height and weight. It does not measure body fat directly, but it gives a simple reference point that many people use to understand whether their weight is broadly within underweight, normal, overweight, or obesity ranges. This calculator uses centimeters and kilograms for fast everyday use.

Enter your height in centimeters and weight in kilograms. The calculator divides weight by height in meters squared and shows the result rounded to one decimal place. For most adults, a BMI below 18.5 is considered underweight, 18.5 to 24.9 is commonly considered normal, 25 to 29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is in the obese range.

BMI is helpful for a first check, but it is not a complete health diagnosis. Athletes, older adults, pregnant people, children, and people with high muscle mass may need a different assessment. Waist measurement, fitness level, medical history, blood markers, and professional advice can give a clearer picture. Use this page as an educational tool, not as a replacement for medical guidance.

BMI formula

BMI = weight in kilograms / (height in meters x height in meters).

Example calculation

If weight is 70 kg and height is 170 cm, height in meters is 1.70. BMI = 70 / (1.70 x 1.70) = 24.2, which is commonly within the normal range.

FAQs

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

No. BMI is a screening estimate and can be less useful for athletes, children, pregnant people, and some older adults.

What units does this calculator use?

It uses centimeters for height and kilograms for weight.

Can BMI diagnose disease?

No. BMI alone cannot diagnose a medical condition. It should be interpreted with other health information.

How often should I check BMI?

Occasional checks are enough for most people. Trends over time are usually more useful than one isolated number.
