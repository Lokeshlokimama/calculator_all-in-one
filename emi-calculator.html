What this EMI calculator does

An EMI, or equated monthly installment, is the fixed amount you pay every month toward a loan. This calculator helps you quickly compare loan choices by showing the estimated monthly payment, total interest cost, and total amount payable over the full tenure. It is useful for home loans, car loans, education loans, personal loans, appliance finance, and other installment-based borrowing.

Use the loan amount as the principal borrowed, enter the annual interest rate offered by your lender, and add the tenure in months. A longer tenure usually reduces the monthly EMI but increases total interest because the loan remains outstanding for more time. A shorter tenure usually raises the monthly EMI but can reduce overall interest. Testing different values before applying can make the repayment plan easier to understand.

The result is an estimate. Actual lender calculations can vary because of processing fees, insurance, prepayment rules, reset dates, rounding, floating interest rates, and taxes. Treat this calculator as a planning tool, then confirm the final repayment schedule with your bank or financial institution. For better decisions, compare EMI with your monthly income, emergency fund, existing commitments, and future expenses.

EMI formula

EMI = P x r x (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1), where P is loan principal, r is monthly interest rate, and n is number of monthly installments.

Example calculation

For a 500,000 loan at 10% annual interest for 60 months, the estimated EMI is about 10,624. Total repayment is about 637,411 and total interest is about 137,411.

FAQs

Is EMI the same every month?

For a fixed-rate loan, EMI generally stays the same. Floating-rate loans can change when the lender resets the rate.

Does a lower EMI always mean a better loan?

No. A lower EMI may come from a longer tenure, which can increase total interest paid over the loan life.

Can I use this for home loans?

Yes. It works for home loans, car loans, personal loans, and other loans with monthly repayment schedules.

Are fees included?

No. Add processing fees, insurance, and taxes separately when comparing lender offers.
