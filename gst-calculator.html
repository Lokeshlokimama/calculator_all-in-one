What this calculator does

The GST Calculator helps calculate Goods and Services Tax for a given amount and rate. It can add GST to a base price or extract GST from a tax-inclusive price, separating the base amount, tax amount, and final total.

The tool performs rate arithmetic only. It does not classify a product or service, decide place of supply, split CGST and SGST, calculate input credits, or determine whether cess or a special rule applies.

When to use it

Use it while preparing invoices, checking supplier bills, comparing tax-inclusive and tax-exclusive prices, planning purchase costs, or learning how GST rates affect final prices.

Select the rate from a current invoice or official classification rather than guessing from the item name. Tax rates and treatment can change, and legal filing should be checked against current CBIC or GST Council material and professional advice.

Formula used

Add GST: GST amount = base amount x rate / 100, final amount = base amount + GST. Remove GST: base = inclusive amount / (1 + rate / 100).

Add mode treats the entered amount as the taxable base. Remove mode treats it as the tax-inclusive total and divides by 1 plus the decimal rate; simply subtracting 18 percent from an inclusive 18-percent price would be incorrect.

Example calculation

If the base price is 1,000 and GST is 18 percent, GST is 180 and final price is 1,180. If 1,180 is inclusive, base is 1,180 / 1.18 = 1,000.

The reverse check is useful: adding 18 percent to 1,000 gives 1,180, and removing 18 percent from the inclusive 1,180 returns the same 1,000 base, apart from display rounding.

Benefits

  • Split taxable and tax value
  • Check invoices
  • Convert inclusive prices
  • Compare GST rates

Use the currency selector only for display. The arithmetic is currency-neutral, so the same values work for rupees or another currency as long as every amount uses the same currency.

How to read the result

Base is the amount before GST, tax is the arithmetic difference attributable to the selected rate, and total is the inclusive amount. Invoice rounding, multiple line items, discounts, reverse charge, and jurisdiction-specific rules can produce a different payable figure.

FAQs

Can this remove GST?

Yes. Use remove mode to split an inclusive price into base and GST.

Does it support every GST rate?

You can enter the rate you need, such as 5, 12, 18, or 28 percent.

Is this tax advice?

No. Confirm tax treatment with official rules or a tax professional.

Can I use this for invoices?

It helps check math, but your invoice must follow local requirements.

Why divide inclusive price?

Tax-inclusive prices must be divided by one plus the tax rate, not simply reduced by the rate.

Inclusive prices, invoice lines, and rounding

GST added to a base and GST removed from an inclusive total are inverse operations. Add mode multiplies the base by the selected rate. Remove mode divides the inclusive total by one plus the rate. This distinction matters because the tax portion is a percentage of the pre-tax base, not the already-taxed total.

Real invoices may calculate tax on each line, after discounts, or at a document level according to applicable rules and software settings. Rounding every line to two decimal places can produce a small difference from calculating on the unrounded total and rounding once. Multiple tax rates, cess, exemptions, reverse charge, place-of-supply rules, and input tax credits are outside this single-rate arithmetic tool.

Use the selected rate only after confirming the current classification from an official source or qualified tax professional. A familiar product name is not enough to determine treatment, and rates can change. The result is best used to check arithmetic on a known rate, compare inclusive and exclusive prices, or spot a likely data-entry error before reviewing the actual invoice.

Assumptions, test cases, and official rates

  • Add case: 1,000 at 18% returns 180 tax and 1,180 total.
  • Remove case: an inclusive 1,180 at 18% returns 1,000 base and 180 tax.
  • Zero-rate case: base and total remain equal and tax is zero.
  • Scope: one amount and one user-selected rate are calculated; invoice classification and filing are outside the tool.

Verify the applicable classification and current rate with the Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs GST rate resources.
