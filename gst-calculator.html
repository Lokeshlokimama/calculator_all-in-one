What this GST calculator does

Goods and Services Tax calculations can be confusing because some prices are quoted before GST while others already include GST. This calculator handles both situations. Choose "Add GST" when you know the base value and want to find the final invoice amount. Choose "Remove GST" when the amount already includes GST and you want to split it into base value and tax value.

The most common GST rates are included in the dropdown, including 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. For business use, always verify the applicable tax slab for the product or service before billing. The calculator is useful for estimates, quotation checks, invoice review, pricing decisions, and understanding how tax changes affect the final customer price.

GST-inclusive removal is not the same as subtracting the tax percentage directly. For example, removing 18% GST from Rs 1,180 does not mean Rs 1,180 minus 18%. The correct base amount is Rs 1,180 divided by 1.18, which gives Rs 1,000. This page shows the base, GST, and final amount separately so the calculation is easier to verify.

GST formula

Add GST: final amount = base amount x (1 + GST rate). Remove GST: base amount = inclusive amount / (1 + GST rate).

Example calculation

If the base amount is Rs 1,000 and GST is 18%, GST amount is Rs 180 and final amount is Rs 1,180. If Rs 1,180 is inclusive, the base amount is Rs 1,000.

FAQs

Can I remove GST from a final bill?

Yes. Select remove GST and enter the tax-inclusive amount.

Which GST rate should I choose?

Select the rate applicable to your product or service. Confirm the slab with official tax guidance if needed.

Why divide by 1 plus rate?

Because the inclusive amount already contains base plus tax. Dividing reverses the tax addition correctly.

Is this a tax filing tool?

No. It is for quick estimates and invoice understanding, not professional tax filing advice.
