What this loan calculator does

This loan calculator helps you understand the cost of borrowing before signing an agreement. Enter the principal amount, annual interest rate, and loan tenure in years. The calculator estimates the monthly payment, total interest, and full repayment amount using a reducing balance style monthly payment formula.

The tool is useful when comparing two loan offers, deciding between a shorter or longer tenure, or checking whether a proposed monthly payment fits your budget. A lower interest rate can reduce total cost, but tenure matters too. Extending the tenure may make monthly payments easier, yet it can increase total interest because the balance stays active longer.

Use the result as a planning estimate. Real loan schedules may include processing fees, insurance, taxes, prepayment penalties, delayed disbursement dates, floating interest changes, or different rounding rules. For a more complete comparison, look at the annual percentage rate, total amount payable, flexibility for part payments, and customer support from the lender. The best loan is not always the one with the lowest monthly payment.

Loan payment formula

Monthly payment = P x r x (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1), where P is principal, r is monthly rate, and n is total months.

Example calculation

For a 300,000 loan at 12% annual interest for 3 years, the estimated monthly payment is about 9,964, with total repayment around 358,713.

FAQs

How is this different from the EMI calculator?

This page uses tenure in years and focuses on overall loan planning. The EMI page uses tenure in months.

Can I use it for personal loans?

Yes. It works for personal, vehicle, education, and other installment loans.

Does it include processing fees?

No. Add fees separately when comparing lender offers.

What happens if interest rate is zero?

The payment is simply principal divided by total months.
