Body size and composition

The BMI Calculator relates adult weight to height and places the result in a screening category. Ideal-weight and body-fat tools answer different questions and use different inputs. BMI does not directly measure body fat, fitness or health, and results may be less informative for children, pregnancy, older adults, highly muscular people or specific clinical conditions.

Energy and nutrition estimates

BMR estimates baseline daily energy use from age, sex, weight and height. Calorie, macro and protein tools extend that estimate using generalized activity or nutrition assumptions. Food composition and individual needs vary, so treat outputs as starting points. A registered dietitian or clinician can account for medical history, medicines, goals and laboratory findings.

Hydration and daily habits

The water-intake calculator provides a simple body-weight and activity estimate. Climate, pregnancy, breastfeeding, illness, medication, kidney or heart conditions and intense exercise can materially change fluid needs. More is not always safer. Follow medical advice when fluid intake has been restricted or when symptoms are present.

Pregnancy date estimates

The due-date tool provides a calendar estimate based on entered dates and a standard pregnancy-length convention. It cannot establish gestational age, fetal health or an exact delivery date. Cycle length, conception timing and clinical measurements can change an estimate, so prenatal decisions belong with a qualified maternity-care professional.

How to use a health estimate safely

Check the requested unit and enter a current measurement rather than a remembered guess. Read the formula description and population limits before interpreting the result. Avoid treating category boundaries as sudden biological changes: a BMI immediately below and above a threshold represents nearly the same measurement even though the label changes. Consider trends, measurement quality and personal context instead of optimizing one isolated number. Do not delay medical care because an online result appears reassuring, and do not begin, stop or change treatment from a calculator output. If a result is unexpected or concerns you, save the entered values and discuss them with an appropriately qualified health professional.
